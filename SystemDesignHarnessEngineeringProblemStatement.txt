Problem Statement
Background

Modern software engineering is undergoing a fundamental shift.

Traditional engineering processes were designed for teams composed entirely of human developers. Today's engineering organizations increasingly consist of humans collaborating with AI coding assistants, autonomous agents, retrieval systems, verification systems, evaluation frameworks, and continuously learning organizational knowledge bases.

Although AI can now generate code, the engineering process surrounding AI remains fragmented.

Organizations typically orchestrate AI agents using ad-hoc prompts, isolated workflows, and disconnected tools.

These systems often suffer from:

Loss of engineering intent
Poor knowledge reuse
Hallucinations
Regression
Weak verification
Missing organizational learning
Inconsistent reasoning
Lack of failure intelligence
No structured unknown discovery
Limited collaboration between specialized agents

As organizations scale, these problems compound.

The limitation is no longer the capability of AI models.

The limitation is the absence of a reusable Engineering Operating System capable of coordinating engineering intelligence.

Objective

Design a model-agnostic Engineering Operating System capable of orchestrating humans, AI agents, engineering workflows, organizational knowledge, and continuous learning into one coherent engineering framework.

The system should enable any future engineering project to inherit the same engineering principles regardless of:

AI model
Programming language
Framework

Final Success Criteria

The generated architecture should satisfy the following:

Technology Agnostic: No dependence on specific languages, frameworks, or AI models.
Composable: Harnesses can be added, removed, or replaced without changing the Kernel.
Explainable: Every decision and interaction can be traced back to intent and evidence.
Learning-Centric: Every execution contributes to organizational knowledge.
Failure-Resilient: Failures become reusable engineering intelligence rather than isolated fixes.
Future-Proof: New AI models, tooling, and engineering practices can be integrated without redesigning the Operating System.
One last recommendation

If you're going to have an AI agent generate the diagrams, don't ask it to "create HLD/LLD diagrams." Ask it to generate an architecture repository.

The repository should contain:

/architecture
│
├── 00_System_Context.drawio
├── 01_HLD.drawio
├── 02_Engineering_Kernel.drawio
├── 03_ERA.drawio
├── 04_Knowledge_Flow.drawio
├── 05_Failure_Intelligence.drawio
├── 06_Unknown_Discovery.drawio
├── 07_Regression_Intelligence.drawio
├── 08_Organizational_Memory.drawio
├── 09_Harness_Interaction.drawio
├── /lld
│     ├── Spec_Harness.drawio
│     ├── Research_Harness.drawio
│     ├── Architecture_Harness.drawio
│     └── ...
└── architecture.md

This approach turns the diagrams into a maintainable, version-controlled architectural asset rather than a single static image, making it much easier for both humans and AI agents to evolve the Harness Engineering Operating System over time.
